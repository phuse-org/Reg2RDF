glycosylated hemoglobin assay. 
(a) identification. a glycosylated hemoglobin assay is a device used to measure the glycosylated hemoglobins (a 1a , a 1b , and a 1c ) in a patient's blood by a column chromatographic procedure. measurement of glycosylated hemoglobin is used to assess the level of control of a patient's diabetes and to determine the proper insulin dosage for a patient. elevated levels of glycosylated hemoglobin indicate uncontrolled diabetes in a patient. 
(b) classification. class ii (performance standards).
