" 177.1670 
polyvinyl alcohol film. 
polyvinyl alcohol film may be safely used in contact with food of the types identified in  176.170(c) of this chapter, table 1, under types v, viii, and ix, in accordance with the following prescribed conditions: 
(a) the polyvinyl alcohol film is produced from polyvinyl alcohol having a minimum viscosity of 4 centipoises when a 4-percent aqueous solution is tested at 20 c. 
(b) the finished food-contact film for use in contact with food types v or ix, when extracted with the solvent characterizing the type of food and under the conditions of time and temperature characterizing its intended use as determined from tables 1 and 2 of  176.170(c) of this chapter, yields total extractives not to exceed 0.078 milligram per square centimeter (0.5 milligram per square inch) of food-contact surface when tested by astm method f34-76 (reapproved 1980), standard test method for liquid extraction of flexible barrier materials, which is incorporated by reference. copies may be obtained from the american society for testing materials, 100 barr harbor dr., west conshohocken, philadelphia, pa 19428-2959, or may be examined at the national archives and records administration (nara). for information on the availability of this material at nara, call 202-741-6030, or go to: http://www.archives.gov/federal_register/code_of_federal_regulations/ibr_locations.html. 
(c) the finished food-contact film shall not be used as a component of food containers intended for use in contact with water.
