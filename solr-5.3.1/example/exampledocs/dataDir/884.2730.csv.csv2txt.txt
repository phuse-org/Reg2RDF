home uterine activity monitor. 
(a) identification. a home uterine activity monitor (huam) is an electronic system for at home antepartum measurement of uterine contractions, data transmission by telephone to a clinical setting, and for receipt and display of the uterine contraction data at the clinic. the huam system comprises a tocotransducer, an at-home recorder, a modem, and a computer and monitor that receive, process, and display data. this device is intended for use in women with a previous preterm delivery to aid in the detection of preterm labor. 
(b) classification. class ii (special controls); guidance document (class ii special controls guidance for home uterine activity monitors).
